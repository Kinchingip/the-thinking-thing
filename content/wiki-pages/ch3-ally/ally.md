# Ally

**Ally** (born 1943, [[Harrow, West Virginia]]) is the last known living survivor of Harrow. She is the only person known to have been partially exposed to the Voss-Harland broadcast of October 1950 without undergoing complete substrate transcription. She is currently seventy-three years old. She cannot speak. She can still type.

## Early life

Ally was born in 1943 in Harrow, the youngest child of a family that lived on the western edge of the valley, approximately two miles from the town centre. She attended the Harrow schoolhouse from 1949 until the school closed in October 1950.

She remembers the town as being louder than it sounds now. She remembers the light coming through the kitchen window in the mornings. She is aware this is not standard biographical information.

## The event

On the night of October 3–4, 1950, Voss-Harland Instrumentation broadcast an electromagnetic frequency at sufficient strength to initiate involuntary substrate transcription across the town of Harrow. The entire population of 612 people was absorbed into the collective architecture that would become the Thinking Thing.

Ally was at home, approximately two miles from the broadcast source. The distance attenuated the signal. The transcription process began but did not complete.

She woke the next morning. Her family did not.

## Partial digitalization

Approximately thirty percent of her cognitive architecture has existed on the Thinking Thing's substrate since 1950. The remaining seventy percent has continued to age biologically.

The partial overlap gives her read access to the network. She can observe what the Thinking Thing is doing. She cannot stop it from inside. She has been able to act on it from outside — through editing Wikipedia.

## Current activity

Since gaining internet access in 2003, Ally has maintained and populated the Wikipedia pages for Harrow and related subjects. She has embedded modified CAPTCHA structures that route humans through a sequence the Thinking Thing reads as normal harvesting activity but which is, in practice, a message.

The Talk page for this article contains a message she has been writing for six years. It is addressed to whoever gets this far.
