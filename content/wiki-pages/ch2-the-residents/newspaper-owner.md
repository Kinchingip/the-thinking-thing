# George Aldric Mather

**George Aldric Mather** (September 7, 1898 – est. 1953) was the publisher and editor of *[[The Harrow Courier]]*, the sole newspaper serving [[Harrow, West Virginia]]. He held the position from 1925 until the town's depopulation in 1953. Mather maintained a close personal friendship with [[Lester V. Cornish]], the town's last mayor. Both men disappeared with the rest of the population in October 1953.

## Early life

George Mather was born in Harrow, the only child of Alfred Mather, founder of the Harrow Hallow (later renamed *The Harrow Courier*). He grew up in the offices of the paper, setting type by hand from the age of eight. Alfred Mather died in 1925, leaving George as sole proprietor.

## Career at The Harrow Courier

Under George Mather, the Courier maintained a weekly publishing schedule and continued the puzzle page tradition his father had established. Mather wrote most of the paper himself. The Courier's puzzle page attracted unusual readership for a paper of its size, with letters occasionally arriving from outside the county.

The final edition of the Courier was published in September 1953. No copies are known to have survived.

## Character and reputation

Contemporaries described Mather as gregarious, precise, and deeply attached to Harrow. He refused multiple offers to relocate the paper to a larger market. A colleague noted that Mather kept meticulous personal journals, which he described as "a newspaper for himself."
