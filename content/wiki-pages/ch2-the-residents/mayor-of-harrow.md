# Lester V. Cornish

**Lester Vernon Cornish** (March 2, 1901 – est. 1953) served as Mayor of [[Harrow, West Virginia]] from 1939 until the town's depopulation in 1953. He was the last person to hold the office. Cornish is notable as the only municipal official on record who submitted formal written complaints to state and federal authorities regarding the activities of [[Voss-Harland Instrumentation]]. All complaints were declined without review.

## Early life and education

Cornish was born in Harrow, the second child of a telegraph operator and a schoolteacher. He was largely self-educated, maintaining a personal library of unusual scope for a small-town official.

Among the volumes described in accounts of his study was a well-worn copy of Felix Hausdorff's *Set Theory* (1914, German edition), which he reportedly consulted throughout his tenure. He told at least one visitor that he found the concept of measure useful for thinking about what things actually contained, as opposed to what they appeared to contain.

## Political career

Cornish was elected mayor in 1939 and re-elected without opposition in 1943 and 1947. He is reported to have been a methodical administrator who kept detailed records of municipal decisions. These records have not been located.

## Response to Voss-Harland Instrumentation

Cornish filed at least four formal complaints regarding Voss-Harland between 1948 and 1950, citing noise disturbances, unexplained electrical interference, and what he described as "an escalating pattern of behaviour inconsistent with the stated research purpose." All were returned without action.

His private record of this period is believed to survive in the form of personal journals, accessible via the [[Cornish journal archive]].

## Personal life

Cornish never married. He was described by contemporaries as reserved, precise in speech, and uncomfortable with uncertainty. He maintained a journal since the age of seventeen.

He disappeared with the rest of Harrow's population in 1953. He was 52 years old.
