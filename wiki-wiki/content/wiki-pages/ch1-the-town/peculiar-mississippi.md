# Peculiar, Mississippi

**Peculiar** is an unincorporated community in Harlan County, Mississippi, United States. As of the 2020 census, its population was recorded as 214, though county records from the same year list only 201 residents by name.

The town is perhaps best known as the site of the 1987 [[Muscipulavirus|Harlan County Outbreak]], a public health event that remains only partially documented in state records. The county health office closed its Peculiar branch in 1989 and has not reopened it.

## Geography

Peculiar sits at the intersection of State Route 44 and an unnamed fire road referred to locally as the Spur. The nearest incorporated town, Delmar, is 34 miles east. There is no direct bus service.

The surrounding area is largely pine flatwoods. A drainage canal running along the western edge of the community is labeled on USGS maps as Cartographer's Creek, though no record of who named it or when has been found.

## History

The community was established in 1911 as a logging camp. It was formally recognized by Harlan County in 1923. Little administrative history survives; the county courthouse fire of 1954 destroyed most records predating it.

[[Henry Liang|Dr. Henry Liang]], a virologist at the University of Mississippi, visited Peculiar in October 2019 as part of a retrospective study on the 1987 outbreak. He has not published findings. His university profile was removed from the department website in March 2020.

## Notable locations

- **[[First Baptist Church of Peculiar|First Baptist Church]]** — the oldest standing structure in the community, built 1931
- **The Spur** — locals report the road extends further than county maps indicate
- **[[The Cartographers|Cartographers' marker]]** — a concrete survey post at the edge of the flatwoods, origin unknown
