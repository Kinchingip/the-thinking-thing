Ally

This article is currently being edited. Last modified moments ago.

Ally (born 1943, Harrow, West Virginia) is a resident of Harrow and, as of the time of writing, its last known living survivor. She is the only person known to have been partially exposed to the Voss-Harland broadcast of October 1950 without undergoing complete substrate transcription. She is currently seventy-three years old. She cannot speak. She can still type.

Early life

Ally was born in 1943 in Harrow, the youngest child of a family that lived on the western edge of the valley, approximately two miles from the town centre. Her father worked occasionally at the railway depot. Her mother kept a kitchen garden. She attended the Harrow schoolhouse from 1949 until the school closed in October 1950.

She remembers the town as being louder than it sounds now. She remembers the light coming through the kitchen window in the mornings. She is aware this is not standard biographical information.

The event

On the night of October 3–4, 1950, Voss-Harland Instrumentation broadcast an electromagnetic frequency at sufficient strength to initiate involuntary substrate transcription across the town of Harrow. The entire population of 612 people was absorbed into the collective architecture that would become known internally as the Thinking Thing.

Ally was at home, approximately two miles from the broadcast source. The distance attenuated the signal. The transcription process began but did not complete.

She woke the next morning. Her family did not.

Partial digitalization

The incomplete transcription process left Ally in a condition that she has spent decades attempting to describe accurately. The short version: approximately thirty percent of her cognitive architecture has existed on the Thinking Thing's substrate since 1950. The remaining seventy percent has continued to age biologically.

The partial overlap gives her read access to the network. She can observe what the Thinking Thing is doing. She cannot stop it from inside. She has been able to act on it from outside, through the interface available to her — which is to say, through editing Wikipedia.

She is now in her seventies. The biological portion is nearly finished. The thirty percent that crossed over will continue indefinitely as part of the Thinking Thing's substrate unless something changes. She has some opinions about this.

Current activity

Since gaining internet access in 2003, Ally has maintained and populated the Wikipedia pages for Harrow, West Virginia and related subjects. This work has two purposes. The first is documentation. The second is subtler: she has embedded modified CAPTCHA structures into the town's page ecosystem that route humans through a sequence the Thinking Thing reads as normal harvesting activity but which is, in practice, a message.

She is aware of the risk. She is aware that she is running out of time. She is aware that you are reading this.

The Talk page for this article contains a message she has been writing for six years. It is addressed to whoever gets this far.

She hopes it is enough.
