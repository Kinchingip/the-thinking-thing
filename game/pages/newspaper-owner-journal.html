October 9, 1947

New outfit moved in east of town. Twelve of them — counted from the window as they drove past the Courier. Lester [Cornish] went out to meet them and came back looking like he'd eaten something bad. He said they had federal paperwork and wouldn't tell him what they were researching. I told him I'd run a story. He said not yet, wait and see. I wrote a small item anyway for the back page. They called it 'an independent research facility.' I called it 'a company with no public record and a twelve-person payroll that arrived on a Tuesday morning without introducing themselves to anyone.'

I ran the second version. Twelve of them.

June 5, 1949

Got a reader submission I can't place — no name, no address, just the puzzle on a plain card. The riddle about the uninvited guest. I know what the answer is. I ran it anyway. Lester was uncomfortable about it. He said it felt like something else, and I said that's exactly why it's a good puzzle.

The answer is hunger. That's what the uninvited guest is. The thing that arrives without asking, grows louder when ignored, reaches without hands. Every living thing knows it because every living thing has it. It's not metaphorical. The author of that puzzle knew exactly what they were saying.

I wish I knew who sent it.

September 7, 1950 — my birthday

Fifty-two years old. Lester brought a bottle of something. We sat in the office and I told him the paper turns sixty-two this year — the Courier, I mean, though it was the Hallow then, the Harrow Hallow. Father started it January 1888. He always said January because he wanted the first issue to be a new year's issue, except it ran two weeks late and came out on the fourteenth. January 14, 1888. I have the original issue in the flat file. It's starting to fade.

We didn't talk about the facility. We didn't talk about the sounds. Lester drank his glass and I drank mine and for a few hours it felt like a normal birthday.

It wasn't.

October 1, 1950

I can hear it in the building now. Constant. A frequency I recognise from the facility — the same thing Lester described to me months ago — but it's here now, inside the walls somehow. The equipment in the composing room is behaving strangely. The type keeps slipping. I reset the same line four times today and each time it read differently when I proofed it. I don't know if I'm misreading or if it's actually changing.

I wrote a front page story. SOMETHING IS WRONG IN HARROW. Two hundred words. I set it in the largest type I have. I don't know if I'll run it.
