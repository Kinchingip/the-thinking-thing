Andy Chan immigrated to the United States in the mid-1970s. He worked as a lab technician at Harrow Digital Labs. He was one of several workers at the facility diagnosed posthumously with radiation-related neurological damage following the events of 1983. He felt alone since his family was back home. Most of the other workers were also immigrants whose families were far away.

Background

Andy Chan arrived in the United States in approximately 1974. He was employed at Harrow Digital Labs as a junior laboratory technician. Personnel records indicate that he shared a dormitory-style residential building with several other workers, most of whom were also recent immigrants. His family remained in Hong Kong. He was survived by his wife, Maria, and son, Jordan.

He spent most of his time working at the lab and came home tired. He is described in coworker accounts as quiet, considerate, and homesick. He kept a journal in Cantonese, portions of which were recovered from his room following facility closure.

Belongings

The following items were recovered from Room 7, Building C, Harrow Digital Labs residential block. Items were catalogued by the Fayette County coroner's office, 1983.

Cassette mixtape

A handwritten cassette tape, unlabelled on one side. The reverse bears a single title in Cantonese.

Journal (translated excerpts)

Translated from Cantonese. Original handwritten pages held by the Fayette County Archive. Spelling and grammatical irregularities preserved from source. Note: handwriting becomes significantly less regular from the third entry onwards.

November 1977  一九七七年十一月

I have been at Harrow Digital Labs for fourteen months. The work is the same every day. Sample preparation, log the numbers, clean the equipment. A woman named Mei Works the same shift — she is from Guangdong, she understands what it means to be far from home. We do not talk about our families much but we understand each other without talking. I wrote to Maria tonight. I told her I was fine. I am mostly fine.

May 1979  一九七九年五月

Jordan turned four. Maria sent a photograph. His hair is longer now. He looks different from how I imagined. I have the photo above my bunk. I look at it before I sleep but my memory of his face and the photo are starting to become the same thing, and that worries me. I keep trying to remember what is underneath the photograph. I cannot.

January 1982  一九八二年一月

my hands have been shaking. I dropted a vial today and the supervisur looked at me for a long time without speaking. Everywhere in the lab there is a sound I cannot place — not loud, just present, like something behind the walls that knows the walls are there. The other workers say they do not hear it. Maybe I am wrong. I want to go home. I do not know when I will go home.

October 1982  一九八二年十月

I cant remember what I was going to write. I started this entry three times. I keep losing the thought midway through. I Read what I wrote last month and I dont recognise the handwriting as mine even though I know it is. Something is wrong. I know there is something wrong. The knowing is the worst part.

December 1982  一九八二年十二月

Everyone is the same now. We are all the same. The ones who have been here longest look like I look now and nobody talks about it. I am frightened of becoming someone who does not talk about it. I am writing this down so that I will remember I was afraid. So that whoever finds this will know.

February 1983  一九八三年二月

I think Maria knows. She hasn't written in two weeks. Maybe the letter is lost. Maybe she knows and does not know what to say. I dont know what I would want her to say either.

March 1983  一九八三年三月

Sometimes I think I can hear Jordan calling for me. I know this is not possible. He is thousands of miles away and I am here and there is a distance between us that is not just miles anymore. I think
